About us

Legal knowledge
shouldn't cost £300/hr.

We exist to make UK law understandable to everyone — not just those who can afford a solicitor.

66%

of people in the UK experience a legal problem every year. Most never get help — because they think it's too expensive, too complicated, or they simply don't know their rights exist.

That's the problem we're here to fix.

KnowYourRights gives everyone access to legal knowledge that used to be locked behind expensive consultations. We write about UK consumer rights, online rights, and social media law — the areas that affect everyday life — in the same way a knowledgeable friend would explain them to you.

What we believe

🧠

Plain English, always

If you need a law degree to understand it, we've failed. Every guide is written for a normal person.

🆓

Free at the core

The guides and tools will always be free. Our AI assistant funds the operation.

✅

Accurate, not sensational

We write what the law actually says and update our guides whenever the law changes.

🇬🇧

UK-specific

Everything covers England and Wales specifically. No generic international advice dressed up as UK law.

What we cover

Consumer rights — the Consumer Rights Act 2015, refund rights, faulty goods, online shopping, digital content, scams, and chargebacks.

Online and social media rights — UK GDPR, Subject Access Requests, the Online Safety Act 2023, content removal appeals, online harassment, defamation, and fake accounts.

We're building out more areas including employment rights, housing, and small business law.

How we make money

The guides and tools are completely free. We keep the site free through donations and will add ways to support it in future.

No ads. No sponsored content. No one paying us to recommend anything.

Important disclaimer

KnowYourRights is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. Our guides provide general legal information about UK law. They are not a substitute for professional legal advice tailored to your specific situation. For complex or high-value matters, please consult a qualified solicitor — find one via solicitors.lawsociety.org.uk.
